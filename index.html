What is Dark Sky Certification?

The International Dark-Sky Association (IDA) is the world's leading authority working to protect the night sky from light pollution. Founded in 1988, IDA designates exceptional places committed to preserving darkness.

Dark Sky Community

Cities and towns that adopt responsible outdoor lighting policies to reduce light pollution and preserve dark skies for residents and wildlife.

Dark Sky Park

Public or private lands with exceptional starry nights, committed to protecting and improving dark sky quality for science, education, and enjoyment.

Dark Sky Reserve

Large regions combining a dark core zone with a surrounding buffer area, often spanning multiple land ownerships working together toward shared goals.

Dark Sky Sanctuary

The most remote and pristine dark sky places on Earth — typically inaccessible, with minimal threat from light pollution and scientifically valuable darkness.

There are over 200 certified Dark Sky Places worldwide across 22 countries.
